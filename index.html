EXPLORATION

Look at the atoms. Are all atoms of
the same size, or is there great
variation in their sizes?

(Click below, to see answer)

There is considerable variation in
the size of the atoms. Hydrogen
atom is very small, while Sodium
atom (Na) is quite large

Click on various atoms, and
observe the elctrons. How are they
placed in the atom?

(Click below, to see answer)

The electrons orbit around the
nucleus, the way planets orbit
around the sun

Electrons orbit around the nucleus, just
as planets orbit around the sun.
However, there is one crucial
difference. Can you find it out? (Hint :
See number of electrons in an orbit for
various atoms)

(Click below, to see answer)

Unlike planetary orbits, more than
one electron is in an orbit. Also,
what is not shown in the animation,
is that the orbits are not all in the
same plane.

What can you say about the number of
electrons in an orbit?

(Click below, to see answer)

The number of electrons in an orbit seems fixed - 2 in first orbit and 8 in the next.Generally, unless an orbit is "filled" to its maximum, the next orbit is not filled. (This is only true of atoms with few electrons)

How are electrons grouped in orbits?

(Click below, to see answer)

Electrons are grouped in orbits in
pairs.

Examine various atoms. Is there any
relationship between the number of
electrons, protons and neutrons?

(Click below, to see answer)

The number of electrons always
equals the number of protons.
There is no exact relationship to
determine the number of neutrons

What is the electric charge of an atom.
Is it positive, negative or neutral. Why?

(Click below, to see answer)

Electrons are negatively charged.
Protons are positively charged. The
number of electrons equals the
number of protons. Hence, the atom
is neutral.

What will happen to the charge of an
atom, if additional electrons are added,
while the proton number is left the
same?

(Click below, to see answer)

Electrons are negatively charged.
When extra electrons are added, the
atom becomes negatively charged and
is called an ion.

What will happen to the charge of an
atom, if some electrons are removed
from the atom?

(Click below, to see answer)

Electrons are negatively charged. The
atom is neutral. If some negative charges
electrons) are removed, the atom
becomes positively charged. The number
of positively charged protons exceeds the
number of negatively charged electrons.

If electrons are removed from an atom,
it becomes positively charged. The
element remains the same. What we get
is an ion of the element.

An atom of Phosporus (or any other
element for that matter) is usually
neutral. If an electron is removed, it
becomes positively charged, and Is
denoted as P+ or P+1 . Similarly, if three
electrons are removed, its charge
equals three proton-charge, and it is
denoted by P+++ or P+3.

What happens if an electron is added to
an atom? Clearly, it becomes a
negatively charged Ion.

If an electron is added to Oxygen (O), it
becomes O- or O-1. If two electrons are
added, it becomes O-- or O-2.

What would happen if a proton is added or removed from an atom?

Important

If a proton is added or removed,
the element does not remain the
same. Each element is
determined by the number of
protons.

An element is determined by the number
of protons in it. No two elements have the
same number of protons. The number of
protons is called the atomic number of that
element. Can you find the atomic numbers
of H, Al and Cu?

(Click below, to see answer)

H   -  1
Al  - 13
Cu - 29

What are the atomic numbers of
Sodium, Phosphorus, Manganese and
Iron?

(Click below, to see answer)

Sodium          - 11
Phosphorus  - 15
Manganese    - 25
Iron                - 26

Greater the number of protons, greater
the atomic number, and greater the
number of electrons. Does that mean
that atomic size always increases with
atomic number?

(Click below, to see answer)

There is a general trend of increasing
atom size with increasing atomic
number. But, it is not universal. Check
these pairs : Copper-Aluminium and
Oxygen-Carbon where atomic number
is higher, but size is smaller.

An element has a shell configuration,
which tells the number of electrons in its various orbits. For Carbon, it is [2,4], which means that there are 2 electrons in first orbit and 4 in the second. What is the shell configuration of Sodium?

(Click below, to see answer)

Sodium (Na) has 2 electrons in the
innermost orbit, 8 in the second, and
finlly 1 in the outermost orbit. Hence,
its shell configuration is [2,8,1]
